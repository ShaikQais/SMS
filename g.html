Badminton

  • A match is made up of three games of 21 points each.
  • A point is scored when the shuttle lands on the opponent's court, passing over the net.
  • A point is scored every time there is a serve.
  • The side that wins a rally adds a point to its score.
  • At 20 all, the side that gains a 2 point lead first wins that game.
  • A two-minute break is allowed between each game.
  • A 60-second break can be taken when the leading score reaches 11 point.
  • In classic-rules badminton in universities, only the server can earn points.
  • For men's / boy's singles and all doubles, only one game of 21 points shall be played.
  • For women's / girl's singles, 15 points shall be played throughout the qualifying rounds

Lawn tennis

  • To win a point, a player must hit a shot before the ball bounces or after the first bounce.
  • If the ball bounces twice, the player with the last shot wins the point.

  • A player must wait for the ball to pass the net and come over to their side of the court.
  • A player cannot step over the net to hit the ball.
  • A ball must land within bounds for play to continue.
  • If a player hits the ball outside of bounds, they lose the point.
  • Players/teams cannot touch the net or posts or cross onto the opponent's side.
  • Players/teams cannot carry the ball or catch it with the racquet.
  • A player or team needs to win two consecutive points immediately after “Deuce” to win the “Game”.
  • There shall be not advertisement on the net, strap band or singles sticks.
  • On each side of the net, at a distance of 21 feet (6.40 m.) from it and parallel with it, shall be drawn the service lines

  • The game is called if one team is ahead by 20 runs after the third inning, 15 runs after the fourth inning, or 10 runs after the fifth inning.

Taekwondo

  • A spinning kick to the opponent's torso is worth two points
  • A kick to the head is worth three points
  • A gam-jeom is the only penalty in taekwondo
  • A gam-jeom is declared when an athlete punches to the face, or punches or kicks below the waist
  • Attacking an opponent with the knee or the head is not allowed
  • If an opponent is knocked to the ground, the referee begins a 10 second count
  • A knockdown occurs if any part of a contestant's body touches the floor apart from the foot
  • There is a mandatory eight-second count before the referee decides whether the bout should continue
  • The winner is the fighter with the most points at the end of the three rounds
  • If both fighters have the same amount of points, then an extra round is fought
  • In this round, the first fighter to score a point is declared the winner
  • A match can also end early if one fighter knocks the other out or if one fighter is disqualified for a rule breach

Tug of War

  • The rope is 33.9 meters long and 1012.5 centimeters in diameter
  • A red ribbon ties the middle of the rope
  • Four and five meters from the red ribbon, the rope should have white and blue ribbons, respectively
  • The center line of the rope should be above the line marked on the ground at the start of the pull
  • Both teams pull the rope
  • The team that pulls the mark on the rope closest to their opponents over the center line wins
  • The game ends when the referee declares one team has successfully pulled past the center
  • The game requires a judge

Table tennis

  • Games are played to 11 points
  • Players alternate serving two points at a time
  • When serving, the ball should be tossed straight up
  • In singles, the serve can land anywhere
  • In doubles, serves must go from right court to right court
  • After serving, every shot should land directly on the opponent's table
  • 1010 is deuce, and the server changes for each point until the difference is two points
  • Finger spins and rubbing the ball against the racket face are illegal
  • Any spin imparted to the ball must come from action of the racket upon impact with the ball
  • The ball shall be struck so that it first drops into the server's court by passing directly over or around the net

Volleybal

  • Players: Each team has six players, with three in the front row and three in the back row. Teams with five players can fill the odd player position if there are at least two men and two women.
  • Hits: A team is allowed a maximum of three hits per side. A player cannot hit the ball twice in a row, except for a block.
  • Serving: The ball can be played off the net during a volley and on a serve.
  • Scoring: The winning team of each rally scores points.
  • Matches: The best of three games wins the match. In non-deciding games, the first team to score 25 points with a minimum two-point advantage wins. In deciding games, the first team to score 15 points with a minimum two-point advantage wins.
  • Substitutions: Each team is allowed 15 substitutions per set.
  • Eligibility: A player who is disqualified for ineligibility will be automatically scratched from the team for the academic year. The team will also be unable to participate in the tournament in the following year.
